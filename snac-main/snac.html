In his younger years, Hardy's father gave him advice that he has been turning over in his mind ever since. Whenever he feels like criticizing anyone, he should remember that all people in the world haven't had the advantages he has. Hardy lives at West Egg, the less fashionable of the two, and his house is an eyesore, but it has a view of the water and the comforting proximity of millionaires. One evening, Hardy drives over to East Egg to see two old friends, Tom Buchanan and Daisy. Tom and Daisy stroll back into the library as if to a vigil beside a perfectly tangible body.
